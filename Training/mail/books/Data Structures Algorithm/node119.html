Brute Strength Method

The brute strength method is based on going through all possible answers to a problem, and choosing the best one. It isn't a terribly clever or efficient method. As an extreme example of a brute strength approach, consider sorting: a brute strength method might try out all possible orderings of the given item until it found one that had the items in the right order. Simple searching algorithms which examine all items in turn can also be viewed as brute strength algorithms. And breadth and depth first search algorithms can be viewed as brute strength, as you are going through every possible path in turn until you find the item you want.

While it isn't a terribly good approach for most problems, it is often possible to finda brute strength approach to a problem, and for some hard problems it may be the best you can manage.
